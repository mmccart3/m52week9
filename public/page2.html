About the Camino de Santiago

The Camino de Santiago, also known as the Way of St. James, is a network of pilgrimage routes that lead to the shrine of the apostle St. James in the Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela in Galicia, Spain.

Pilgrims from all walks of life and various faiths have embarked on this spiritual journey for centuries. The most popular route, the Camino Frances, traverses the stunning landscapes of northern Spain.
